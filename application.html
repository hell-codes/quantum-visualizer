Applications of Quantum Mechanics

Quantum mechanics is not only a theoretical field. It powers many technologies that shape the modern world.

Scanning Tunneling Microscope

STM uses quantum tunneling to detect extremely small distances between a sharp tip and a conductive surface. Electrons tunnel through the gap allowing scientists to image individual atoms.

Semiconductor Devices

Quantum tunneling is important in semiconductor devices such as tunnel diodes and modern transistors. It enables fast switching in integrated circuits.

Quantum Computing

Quantum computers use quantum states such as superposition and entanglement to perform computations that are extremely difficult for classical computers.

Nuclear Fusion in Stars

Quantum tunneling allows protons inside stars to overcome the Coulomb barrier and fuse together, producing the energy that powers stars like the Sun.
