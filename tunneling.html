Quantum Tunneling Simulation

Quantum tunneling is a phenomenon where a particle can pass through a potential barrier even when its energy is lower than the barrier height. In classical physics this would be impossible, but in quantum mechanics particles behave like waves and part of the wave can penetrate the barrier.

Tunneling Concept

Transmission Probability T ≈ e−2κa

κ = √(2m(V − E)) / ħ V = Barrier Height E = Particle Energy a = Barrier Width

Simulation Controls

Energy = 20

Higher energy increases tunneling probability.

Concept Explanation

When a quantum particle encounters a potential barrier, its wavefunction does not abruptly stop. Instead, the wave decays exponentially inside the barrier.

If the barrier is thin enough, the wave can emerge on the other side, meaning the particle has tunneled through.
